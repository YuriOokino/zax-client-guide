2.

Know your CMS

Pages

The Pages panel is where you'll find all the static content of your website. This includes:

Static pages: these are pages I built outside of your Collections. These include your homepage, About page, Services/Products, Contact, etc.

Dynamic pages: these are pages (and sometimes sections within pages) generated from your Collections. On your site these are Privacy Policy Terms of Use and the FAQ section on the homepage.

Manage page settings

When you hover your cursor over a page in the list, you'll see a button labeled "Settings" appear. There, you can manage vital page settings like SEO meta title and description, Open Graph title and description, and Open Graph image.

SEO Settings

SEO stands for "search engine optimization." It's the practice of building and improving websites in order to improve their chances of being found through search engines like Google, Bing, etc.

In each page's settings, you can create and edit the following page-specific SEO settings:

Meta title: A page's title plays a very important role in its visibility and performance in search engines, both because it a) tells search engines what the page is about and b) is what search engine users will click on to reach your site. Be sure to include the types of words people use to describe your business in the title. Ideally, a page's title should be about 55 to 60 characters long.

Meta description: The description allows you to add a little more detail about your page (about 150 characters of it). It's important for search, but not nearly as important as the title.

The Search Result Preview sections shows you what the content you've entered will look like on a Google search results page. If any of your text gets cut off in the preview, you'll want to trim your text to fit.

Open Graph (OG) Settings

Open Graph settings are very similar to SEO settings, except that they're used by social networks like Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn.

In most cases, you can just tick the checkbox to use the same content as your SEO title and description. But feel free to customize it, if you'd like.

Open Graph also allows you to specify an image to be displayed along with your title and description.

3.

Create and manage content

Your site uses Webflow's CMS, which includes two types of content: static Pages and dynamic Collections. You'll manage both through the Webflow Editor.

All changes—whether to text, images, links, or Collection items—must be published before they appear live on the site.

Pages (Static Content)‍

These include your Homepage, Contact page, and Acquisition Criteria page.

You can update existing text, images, buttons, and links

You cannot add or remove sections—only edit what's already there

Collection Lists (Dynamic Content)

Content from Collections can only be edited from the Collections tab. Changes made directly to a Rich Text block or section on the page won't override the Collection content.

Update the Privacy Policy / Terms of Use

‍These pages are made with Rich Text fields, allowing you to style content (headings, lists, quotes, links, etc.) without needing classes or code.

Each item follows the same structure:

Title: The main heading at the top of the page (e.g., "Privacy Policy")

Slug: Automatically generated based on the title

Last updated: Tracks when the document was last modified

Content: The legal body. You can add text elements but cannot insert images or videos.

Update FAQ Section

‍To create a new FAQ:

Click + New in the FAQ Collection

Fill in each field (pay attention to any help text)

Name: Internal reference only (not shown on site)

Slug: Created from the Name

Question: The visible FAQ question

Answer: The visible FAQ answer

Order: Controls the display order (lowest appears first)

Show/Hide: Use this toggle to control if the item is shown on the live site

To hide the entire FAQ section, make sure all toggles are set to "off." If any are on, the section will appear and display only those active items.
